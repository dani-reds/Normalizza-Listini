Offer only valid upon knowledge of exact dimensions and weights prior to booking.
Overwidth cargo on flat containers needs to be loaded centralised both length and width wise up to a
maximum length of 540cms/20flat and 1150/40flat. This to avoid cargo stowage against the endwalls of
the flat (min. 30cms away) and to enable us to still load your cargo between the cellguides on board of
the vessel. If stowage instruction is not adhered to, shipment cannot proceed and offer is considered as
invalid.
Proper lashing & securing is required and subject to masters approval.
Steel plates lashing pre-lashed
Please ensure that the lashing of steel plates, pre-lashed on flatracks is arranged always according to the
following Hapag-Lloyd standards:
* Antislip material to be used between each plate, best rubber plates.
* Plates to be blocked towards the end walls and in between. Instead of blocking, diagonal lashings
could be used to stop the steel plates from moving in fore and aft direction. If steel plates have different
lengths, a block stow with timber should be arranged, this could be secured with diagonal lashings.
* Try to avoid steel plates with different width on one flat.
* Plates with different width must be secured separately; or space to be filled out with timber to have the
same width as the other plates in the bundle.
* Thin plates up to 15mm thickness with the same width should be pre-bundled by steel bands and
should be handled as one piece (max. 10 tons)
* Smaller plates on top of wider plates must be secured using loop lashing method. (One lashing is
secured on the left side of the flat runs under the plate, and back over the top to the same lashing point
on the left. * A second corresponding lashing from the right side, under the cargo, over and back to the
right lashing point also to be used.) or space to be filled out with timber so that the upper plate have the
same width as the lower plate.
* Corner protections are to be used between plates and lashing material.
